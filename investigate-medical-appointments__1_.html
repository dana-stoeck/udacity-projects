Introduction¶

I have selected the No-Show Appointments data set to analyze. This data set includes information for roughly 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment.

In my analysis I plan to explore how each factor effects the rate of patients not attending their appointments.
More specifically what external factors make a difference in the No-Show rate and what medical factors make a difference in the No-Show rate.

The data set includes the following variables:

PatientId: the identification number of each patient
AppointmentID: the identification of each appointment
Gender: Male or Female
AppointmentDay: the day of the actual appointment
ScheduledDay: the day someone called or registered for the appointment
Age: how old the patient is at the time of the appointment
Neighbourhood: where the hospital is that the appointment takes place
Scholarship: whether or not the patient is on Brazil's social welfare program
Hipertension: whether or not the patient suffers from Hypertension
Diabetes: whether or not the patient suffers from Diabetes
Alcoholism: whether or not the patient suffers from Alcohol dependency
Handcap: whether or not the patient is handicapped
SMS_received: whether or not the patient recieved 1 or more reminder messages
No-show: whether or not the patient was at their appointment

Conclusions¶


Roughly 20% of patients to not attend the appointments they scheduled. The data set provides information about each appointment such as the age of the patients, where the appointment was located, and a few possible diseases that each patient suffers from. No one factor can be pointed to as a cause for a patient not to show up, but there are a few factors that are correlated with higher or lower rates of no-show appointments.


Patients who receive reminders about their appointments are more likely not to attend the appointment.
Patient no-show rates are skewed to the right and vary by age.
Appointments on Monday, Friday, and Saturday are somewhat more likely not to be attended.
Patients on Brazil's social welfare system are somewhat more likely not to attend their appointments than those not on social welfare.
Patients suffering from Hypertension are somewhat more likely to show up to their appointments than those without the illness.
Patients suffering from Diabetes are somewhat less likely to show up to their appointments than those without the illness.
Patients suffering from Alcohol Dependency are somewhat more likely to show up to their appointments than those without the illness.


Without more information from the patient as to the cause of them not showing up to their appointment one cannot conclude the reason behind it, but one can make a prediction that a patient who receives a reminder, has an appointment on Saturday, is on social welfare, and suffers from Diabetes is less likely than a patient without those factors to attend their appointment.
